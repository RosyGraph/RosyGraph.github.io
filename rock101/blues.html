I IV V

Music Concepts Covered:

  • I, IV, V
  • 12 bar blues
  • Improvisation

Learning Objectives:

  • To understand the I, IV, V concept
  • To understand the 12 bar blues
  • To learn how to improvise

Twelve-bar Blues:

  • Make 12 bar chart on whiteboard
  • Tell them we are playing a blues song in A
  • Question: what are the I, IV, and V notes in A?
  • Explain each bar
  • Have them play through the progression several times in different keys
  • Most students have trouble repeating the structure, have them count through the measures to know where the "one" is. Have the singer count measures aloud.

Twelve-bar Blues Improv:

  • Have the kids make up a twelve bar blues song
  • Ask for a 6 syllable phrase that involves an activity ("I woke up this morning")
  • Ask for a 5 syllable phrase that describes another activity or feeling ("and I feel so bad")
  • Ask for a final 9 syllable phrase to answer one of the other lines
  • Write the phrases on the chart
  • Get different phrases
  • When you come up with a different song, change the key
  • Have guitarists improvise
