Philosophy of Religion:

• Causal version of the Cosmological argument (CAUSAL) ◦ There are things which came into existence ◦ Whatever comes into existence is caused to exist by something else ◦ There cannot be an infinite series of past causes ◦ Therefore there was a first cause (God) • Contigency version of the Cosmological argument (CONA) ◦ Form

◦ The physical universe exists ◦ Either the physical universe is necessary or contingent ◦ The physical universe is not necessary ◦ Therefore the physical universe is contingent ◦ The physical universe's existence depends on either ▸ Some other contingent beings ▸ Or a necessary being ◦ PU's existence does not depend on some other contingent beings ◦ Therefore the universe depends on a necessary being (God)

◦ Contigent vs necessary

◦ x is a contingent being iff x exists and could fail to exist ◦ x is a necessary being iff x exists and could not fail to exist

◦ Third and fifth premise

◦ Argument for 3 ▸ Everything in PU is contingent ▸ Something that is made of things of a kind is of that kind ▸ Therefore PU is contingent ◦ Argument for 5 ▸ If PU depends on contingent beings for its existence without needing an eventual appeal to a necessary being, then everything must be contingent ▸ If everything is contingent, then there was a time when nothing existed ▸ If there was a time when nothing existed, then nothing exists now ▸ Something exists now ▸ Therefore PU's existence does not depend on some other contingent beings

• Anselm's version of the Ontological Argument ◦ Form

◦ A being is God iff it is the greatest conceivable being ◦ God exists in thought but not in reality (reductio assumption) ◦ Existence in both reality and in thought is greater than existence in thought alone ◦ We can conceive of God's existence in reality and in thought ◦ Therefore we can conceive of a being which is greater than God ◦ Therefore we can conceive of a being which is greater than the greatest conceivable being (contradiction) Therefore our reductio assumption is false

◦ Distinction between existence in thought and reality ◦ "Perfection-making property" ◦ Guanilo's objection - perfect island ◦ Kant-Frege objection

◦ Form (modus tollens) ▸ If existence is a perfection-making property, then existence is a property ▸ Existence is not a property ▸ Thus existence is not a perfection-making property ◦ Negative existential

• Teleological Argument - William Paley ◦ Form

◦ The systems in biological nature are complex, purposive and intricately-func ◦ Artifacts are complex, purposeive and intricately-func and were designed ◦ Therefore the systems in biological nature are designed ◦ If the world is designed, the designer is the JCM God ◦ Therefore JCM God exists

◦ Terms

◦ Complexity - The more kinds of parts, the more complex ◦ Purposive - Serving as a means to which an end is met ◦ Intricately-functioning - Each part functions individually to serve an end

◦ Objections to premise three

◦ Argument cannot rule out many scenarios - watchmaker God, etc ◦ Comparison is not fair ◦ Darwin's evolution

• Logical Problem of Evil ◦ Form

◦ If evil exists in the world, then God does not exist ◦ Evil exists in the world ◦ Therefore God does not exist

◦ Denial of premise two - "evil is like the hole in a donut" ◦ The Argument from Free Will

◦ The existence of evil is not to be ascribed to God, but rather free will ◦ J.L. Mackie's response ▸ It is logically possible that God created humans such that ▹ They have free will and... ▹ They never choose to do evil ▸ Human's don't actually choose evil most of the time

◦ "Best of All Possible Worlds" response

◦ "All is well from God's perspective" ◦ All evil works together to create a world that is the best it could be ◦ Quilt analogy
